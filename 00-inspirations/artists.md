There is something in between eudaimonia and hedonism.

Oishi called it psychological richness in his life in three dimensions.

So I have this other theory that there are different people: some are service workers and they do the same thing every day happily, but the same thing. There are production workers that work on projects and have long-term goals and things where they succeed or fail quite differently than the day-to-day service worker.

So perhaps in between this space that is psychological readiness, there is the idea of the artist, a person who works every day like a service worker. At the same time, from time to time, perhaps creating a work of great beauty or something innovative. In other words, from time to time they are not service workers, but they are productive people.Perhaps they go back and forth between one role and the other, but they are not service workers and they're not project workers as something else.
