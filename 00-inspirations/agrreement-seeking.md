# Agreement-Seeking

I find that a lot of my discussions with people are about what I am seeking, which is some kind of agreement. An agreement that

* we're both seeing something from the same lens
* Both on the same page about a certain topic

If I question and don't find an agreement on the particular point I'm topping, I will edit the topic down to a more overall or basic topic.

>I will even go to a well. It seems that we can't agree whether the interest rate is too high or too low. But can we at least agree on the number of forks on the table right now?

There are a number of issues with this way of dealing with people.

I usually forget to explain this process to somebody else. And they get lost in my argument switching.

It's mostly some kind of approval-seeking behavior on my part.
