# Natural Birth

The following thoughts are derived memories of giving birth to three daughters, starting with Alix in 1986 born in Hong Kong.

In Hong Kong, we were far away from family and traditional thoughts. We did our own research. We read books, talked to friends, and attended classes.

There was virtually no sort of giving birth at home. The frequently talked about topic was "natural birth".

## Babies in Hong Kong

If I remember correctly, our first two daughters spent Their infancy is sleeping in between the two of us. Because of the fear of SIDS, our third daughter may have slept in a baby carrier in between our pillows.

They pretty much slept in our bed until the next order arrived. This pattern was inherited from my wife's mother.

Living in Hong Kong gave us some glorious visions of how billions of people have raised their children In order to be smart, well-adjusted, nice people

The simple lesson is: infants are given absolute security. They're almost always touching somebody in somebody's arms, carried in a cloth draped over the grandmother's shoulders.

The bonus is that the child could go everywhere, down into the metro, slip into restaurants down narrow alleys, Get up several flights of stairs, We could go anywhere, and baby was with us.
